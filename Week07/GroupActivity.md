# **Scenario A**: The Algae-Plastics Dilemma

**The Situation**

You are a team of product managers at a major consumer goods company (Uni-clothes). Your brand is under immense public pressure to reduce its massive plastic footprint.

**The Catch**

Your R&D team has developed an innovative bio-plastic made from ocean algae. However, because the technology is new, production is **35% more expensive than traditional plastic**, and the supply chain is highly unstable. Moving completely to this eco-friendly material means you will have to raise prices on your everyday products, hitting lower-income families hardest during an economic downturn.

# **Scenario B**: The Bias in the Hiring AI

**The Situation**

You are a team of data scientists at a major tech firm. Your company has been piloting an internal AI tool to screen the thousands of resumes you receive for engineering roles. The tool has successfully cut the HR screening time down by 80%.

**The Catch**

During a routine audit, your team discovers that the AI is exhibiting a statistical bias against female applicants. Because it was trained on the company’s past 15 years of hiring data (a period when the tech industry was overwhelmingly male), the algorithm penalized resumes that included phrases like "women's chess club captain" or graduates from all-female colleges.

# **Scenario C**: The Scarce Vaccine Allocation

**The Situation**

You are a team of public health administrators on a hospital ethics board. A severe outbreak of a respiratory virus has hit your city. An effective vaccine has just been approved, but your hospital has only received 1,000 doses for a community of 10,000 people.

**The Catch**

You must decide how to distribute this first wave of vaccines. You have two primary groups at risk:

1. **Frontline Healthcare Workers**
   500 doctors, nurses, and respiratory therapists who treat the sick daily.
2. **Vulnerable Elderly Patients**
   2,000 high-risk citizens over the age of 70 who are statistically the most likely to die if they catch the virus.
