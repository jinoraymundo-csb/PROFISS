# AI Ethics

## I. Global Standards & Institutional Frameworks

- [UNESCO's Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence](https://www.unesco.org/en/artificial-intelligence/recommendation-ethics) The first global standard on AI ethics adopted by 193 Member States. It provides a deep dive into core values like human dignity, social justice, and explicit policy recommendations regarding **proportionality**, **safety**, and **accountability**
- [OECD AI Principles](https://ischool.syracuse.edu/what-is-responsible-ai/) An international standard that sets guidelines for public policy, emphasizing inclusive growth, sustainable development, human-centered values, transparency, and traceability.
- [NIST AI Risk Management Framework](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework) A highly influential framework used by organizations globally to manage risks (including bias and lack of explainability) throughout the AI lifecycle.

## II. Industry Applications & Implementation Guides

- [Syracuse University's Guide to Responsible AI](https://ischool.syracuse.edu/what-is-responsible-ai/) A strong academic-to-industry translation detailing the "**Assess** -> **Implement** -> **Audit**" operational lifecycle. It details practical corporate measures like setting up independent ethics review boards and building ethical checks into the early stages of model design.
- [IBM's Pillars of Trust & Socio-Technical Practice](https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/responsible-ai) Focuses on AI as a "socio-technical practice" that marries human oversight with tooling. It offers excellent language on operationalizing principles through cross-functional AI ethics teams, model documentation, and technical bias detection metrics (e.g., assessing demographic parity).
- [Snowflake's Principles and Practices for Trustworthy Systems](https://www.snowflake.com/en/artificial-intelligence/ai-governance/responsible-ai/) Excellent for technical implementation examples. It breaks down the realities of algorithmic bias (how proxy variables bypass simple field removals) and defines what transparency looks like practically via Model Cards and data sheets.
- [ISO's Guide to Managing the AI Ethics Debate](https://www.iso.org/artificial-intelligence/responsible-ai-ethics) Differentiates between abstract "AI Ethics" (philosophical/societal goals) and "Responsible AI" (tactical implementation, risk mitigation, and corporate governance).

## III. Advanced Context: "The Black Box" & Agentic AI

- [Infosys BPM Analysis on Agentic AI Ethics](https://www.infosysbpm.com/blogs/generative-ai/agents-in-ai-ethical-considerations-accountability-and-transparency.html) Focuses on the unique ethical dilemmas of autonomous AI agents. It highlights how the complexity of modern architectures creates "black box" transparency issues, and explains how distributed development blurs traditional lines of human accountability.
- [WJARR's Study on Algorithmic Bias and Business Analytics](https://wjarr.com/sites/default/files/fulltext_pdf/WJARR-2025-0571.pdf) A peer-reviewed paper that analyzes real-world harms in high-stakes fields (like credit lending, law enforcement, and recruitment). It is an excellent source for academic citations concerning **Explainable AI (XAI)** techniques and data ethics frameworks.

---

| Core Principle | The Ethical Issue | The Practical Solution |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bias Mitigation | Historical inequalities embedded in training data create discriminatory feedback loops. | Diversifying datasets; utilizing fairness testing metrics (like equal opportunity math models); regular third-party bias audits. |
| Transparency | Complex architectures cause opaque "black box" decisions; proprietary restrictions shield evaluation. | Publishing Model Cards/Data Sheets; implementing Explainable AI (XAI); explicit user disclosures when AI handles automated decisions. |
| Accountability | Distributed development and autonomous systems make it difficult to attribute liability when errors occur. | Establishing named human owners for specific systems; building cross-functional ethics review boards; maintaining audit decision logs. |
